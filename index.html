Pickleball for Beginners: Everything You Need to Know Before Your First Game
Pickleball · March 5, 2026 · 4 min read

Pickleball is the fastest growing sport in India right now — and Match-Box Mysore has four premium courts waiting for you. If you've never played before, here's everything you need to walk in with confidence.

What Is Pickleball?

Think of it as a mix between tennis, badminton, and table tennis. Played on a smaller court with a solid paddle and a plastic ball with holes, it's easy to pick up but endlessly fun to master. Games are typically played to 11 points, win by 2.

What to Bring

Just yourself and some sports shoes. Match-Box provides paddles and balls for beginners. Wear comfortable athletic wear and bring water — you'll work up a sweat faster than you expect.

Your First Game

Don't worry about technique on day one. Focus on getting the ball over the net and staying out of the kitchen (the non-volley zone near the net). Everything else comes with reps. Our coaches are also available if you want structured guidance.

Ready to Play?

Book a pickleball slot at any of our three Mysore locations via WhatsApp. Courts fill up fast on evenings and weekends, so book ahead!

5 Box Cricket Strategies That Will Make You Unbeatable at Match-Box
Box Cricket · February 10, 2026 · 3 min read

Box cricket is fast, chaotic, and brilliant. The smaller pitch and shorter overs mean every ball matters. Here are five strategies that separate winners from everyone else on the Match-Box turf.

1. Rotate the Strike

Don't go for sixes every ball. Singles and twos add up fast in short formats and keep the scoreboard ticking without the risk of losing wickets.

2. Bowl to the Boundaries

In box cricket, the side boundaries are tight. Bowl wide yorkers to force batters into awkward angles — the walls become your extra fielder.

3. Set Your Field Early

Before the first ball, discuss fielding positions with your team. Box cricket moves too fast to reorganize mid-over. Cover the straight boundary and the square leg — those are the most common scoring zones.

4. Use Your Last Over Wisely

Save your best bowler for the final over, not the first. The pressure of the last few balls is where games are won and lost.

5. Communicate Constantly

Call for every run. Box cricket run-outs are extremely common due to the tight dimensions. Loud, clear communication between batters is the single most underrated skill in the format.
